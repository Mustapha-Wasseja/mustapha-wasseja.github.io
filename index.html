About

My work studies how economic outcomes are shaped by spatial dependence, networks, climate shocks, institutional change, and policy design. I use spatial, network, Bayesian, panel-data, and machine-learning methods to evaluate policy questions and build reproducible empirical tools.

My doctoral research in Economics examined applied spatial econometrics in policy analysis, including monetary policy deliberation, post-Brexit economic connectedness, multi-country macroeconomic models, and climate impacts on agricultural productivity.
